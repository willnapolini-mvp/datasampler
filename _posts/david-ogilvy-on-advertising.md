# OGILVY ON ADVERTISING — Data Sampler Summary

## Core Insight
> Advertising that sells is research-driven, customer-focused, and built on clear promises—not cleverness alone.

---

## Representative Samples: 4

### Sample 1: The "18 Miracles of Research"
**Scenario**: Before launching the Hathaway Shirt campaign, Ogilvy's team researched reader habits, tested headlines, and discovered that an eyepatch-wearing model created intrigue without confusing the product message.  
**Pattern**: Research isn't optional homework—it's the engine of effective advertising. Test before you invest; let data shape creativity, not replace it.

### Sample 2: Headline = Promise + Brand
**Scenario**: An ad for Rolls-Royce opened with: "At 60 miles an hour, the loudest noise in this new Rolls-Royce comes from the electric clock." The headline named the brand, made a specific promise, and invited reading.  
**Pattern**: Your headline must do three things: include the brand, state a benefit, and compel further reading. Skip any one, and you lose up to 80% of potential impact.

### Sample 3: Long Copy + Facts Sell
**Scenario**: Ogilvy's direct-mail campaign for a financial service used 2,000+ words of specific claims, testimonials, and data—outperforming short, "punchy" alternatives by 3:1 in conversions.  
**Pattern**: When the product is complex or high-consideration, detailed, factual copy builds trust and drives action. Facts sell better than hype because they reduce buyer risk.

### Sample 4: Brand Personality > Generic Polish
**Scenario**: The Hathaway Shirt's eyepatch wasn't about the shirt's fabric—it gave the brand a mysterious, sophisticated character that competitors lacked, creating instant differentiation.  
**Pattern**: Branding is personality engineering. A distinctive, consistent character makes your product memorable and defensible in crowded markets.

---

## Key Generalizations

| Concept | Core Truth |
|---------|-----------|
| Research First | Creativity without insight is decoration; insight without creativity is invisible. |
| Promise-Driven Copy | Every ad is a contract: headline makes the promise, body proves it, close asks for action. |
| Simplicity + Specificity | Simple design + specific claims = clarity. Clarity converts. |

**Rule-of-Thumb**:  
`Effective Ad = (Research × Big Idea) + (Clear Promise × Specific Proof)`

---

## Conclusion
Apply Ogilvy's method by starting every project with audience research, then crafting a headline that names your brand and promises a tangible benefit. Support it with factual, benefit-driven copy—even if it's long—and infuse your brand with a distinctive personality. Test, measure, and iterate—because in advertising, what gets measured gets improved.
