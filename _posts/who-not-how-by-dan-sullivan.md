# Who Not How — Data Sampler Summary

## Core Insight
> Stop asking "How can I do this?" and start asking "Who can help me do this?"—this single mindset shift unlocks exponential growth by leveraging collective genius over solitary effort.

---

## Representative Samples: 4

### Sample 1: Michael Jordan's Championship Turnaround
**Scenario**: Despite being the greatest player alive, Jordan won zero championships in his first six NBA seasons. Only after Scottie Pippen joined, Phil Jackson became coach, and Tim Grover trained him did the Bulls win six titles.
**Pattern**: Even elite performers hit ceilings alone. Greatness compounds when you surround yourself with Whos who complement your strengths and cover your gaps.

### Sample 2: Sharon Duncan's 500-Hour Freedom
**Scenario**: An overwhelmed entrepreneur hired a practice manager to handle administrative tasks she disliked. Result: 500 hours/year freed instantly—time she reinvested in family and high-value strategy.
**Pattern**: Delegation isn't a cost; it's a multiplier. Freeing your time from low-leverage work creates space for vision, creativity, and relationships that drive real growth.

### Sample 3: Paul Heiss's India Factory Sprint
**Scenario**: When tariffs spiked costs, Heiss needed a factory in India. Instead of asking "How do I build a factory overseas?" he asked "Who can help me?" He used an Impact Filter to clarify his need, contacted the Indian Consul General, and launched operations in 5 months.
**Pattern**: Clarity attracts capability. When you define the *what* and *why* precisely, the right Who emerges—and execution accelerates beyond solo effort.

### Sample 4: Benjamin Hardy's Podcast Scaling
**Scenario**: For his first book, Hardy booked podcasts manually—exhausting and limited. For book two, he hired Connie with one instruction: "Get me on 200 podcasts." She delivered 600 the next year.
**Pattern**: Systematic delegation scales impact. Hand off repeatable processes to specialists so you can focus on your unique genius—your message, vision, and strategic decisions.

## Key Generalizations

| Concept | Core Truth |
|---------|-----------|
| Procrastination Signal | Delay often means "This isn't your task"—not "You're lazy." Use it as a cue to find a Who. |
| 80% Rule | Perfect is the enemy of progress. Get work to 80%, then pass it to a Who to refine and finish. |
| Self-Expansion Model | You grow not just through skill-building, but through relationships that add new perspectives and resources. |

## Formula
> **The Who Filter**: Before starting any project, ask: (1) What result do I want? (2) Why does it matter? (3) Who already knows how to deliver this? If you can't name a Who, you're not ready to start.

---

## Conclusion
Apply Who Not How by auditing your weekly tasks: circle anything someone else could do 80% as well, then delegate one today. Use the Impact Filter to clarify your next big goal, then ask "Who can help?" instead of "How do I?" Freedom isn't earned by doing more—it's unlocked by collaborating smarter. Your vision expands when you stop being the bottleneck.
