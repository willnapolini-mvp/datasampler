# To Sell Is Human — Data Sampler Summary

## Core Insight
> Everyone sells—roughly 40% of work time involves "non-sales selling"—and influence now flows from service, not pressure, guided by three new ABCs: Attunement, Buoyancy, Clarity.

---

## Representative Samples: 4

### Sample 1: The Empty Chair (Attunement)
**Scenario**: Jeff Bezos requires an empty chair in key Amazon meetings to represent the customer—the most important person not in the room. This forces the team to view decisions through the buyer's eyes, not internal metrics.
**Pattern**: *Power increases when you reduce it*. Perspective-taking—not authority—drives influence today. When information is democratized, the seller who understands the buyer's context wins.

### Sample 2: "Can We Fix It?" (Buoyancy)
**Scenario**: Before a tough pitch, instead of affirmations ("I'm unstoppable!"), ask yourself interrogative self-talk: "Can I solve their problem?" Research shows question-based self-talk outperforms declarative pep talks by triggering solution-oriented thinking.
**Pattern**: *Questions unlock agency*. Interrogative self-talk primes your brain to generate strategies, not just hope. Buoyancy isn't blind optimism—it's resilient problem-framing.

### Sample 3: The Hidden $300 Leak (Clarity)
**Scenario**: A window salesperson doesn't pitch features. Instead, they show a homeowner a thermal scan revealing $300/year in energy loss through old windows—making an invisible problem visible and urgent.
**Pattern**: *Problem-finding beats problem-solving*. In an age of abundant information, value shifts from answering questions to revealing the right questions. Clarity is curating insight, not dumping data.

### Sample 4: The Patient Photo Effect (Service)
**Scenario**: Hospital radiologists found 5× more incidental issues in scans when shown a photo of the patient's face. Humanizing the "customer" triggered deeper care and attention—not because of incentives, but empathy.
**Pattern**: *Service is a cognitive tool*. Connecting work to a human outcome isn't just noble—it sharpens performance. When you see the person, you see more.

---

## Key Generalizations

| Concept | Core Truth |
|---------|-----------|
| The Information Shift | When buyers have equal access to data, influence flows to those who offer perspective, not just products. |
| Rejection Immunity | Resilience isn't avoiding "no"—it's framing setbacks as temporary, specific, and external (optimistic explanatory style). |
| The Service Paradox | The more you focus on improving the other person's life, the more effectively you move them. |

## Formula
> **The 1-Word Pitch Test**: If you can't summarize your offer in one word (Volvo = *safety*, Toyota = *reliability*), your message lacks clarity. Force distillation to expose your core value.

---

## Conclusion
Modern influence isn't about closing—it's about connecting. Apply the new ABCs: *Attune* by reducing your power to amplify perspective; *Stay buoyant* with interrogative self-talk and optimistic framing; *Clarify* by revealing hidden problems, not just pitching solutions. Whether persuading a client, colleague, or child, remember: people don't buy what you do—they buy why it matters to them. Start with their chair, not your script.
