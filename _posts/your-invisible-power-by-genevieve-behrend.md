# Your Invisible Power — Data Sampler Summary

## Core Insight
> Visualization isn't wishful thinking—it's creative law: your mental pictures, held with faith and will, attract the conditions that manifest your desires through the Universal Mind.

---

## Representative Samples: 4

### Sample 1: The $20,000 Visualization
**Scenario**: Behrend wanted to study with mentor Thomas Troward but lacked funds. For six weeks, she nightly visualized counting twenty $1,000 bills, affirming "My mind is a center of Divine operation." The money arrived—unsolicited—exactly as pictured.
**Pattern**: *Clarity + consistency = manifestation*. Visualization works not by forcing outcomes, but by aligning your inner state with the creative law that shapes external reality.

### Sample 2: The Doctor's Wife's Dream
**Scenario**: Born in poverty, a young woman cherished a mental image of marrying a physician. She took a job as a nursemaid in a doctor's household; years later, after his wife died, he married her. Later, they visualized owning a fruit farm—and did.
**Pattern**: *Stationary pictures attract moving circumstances*. A clear, emotionally charged mental image acts as a magnetic nucleus, drawing compatible people and opportunities into alignment.

### Sample 3: General Foch's Victory Picture
**Scenario**: After France's defeat in 1870, General Foch visualized himself leading a victorious army against Germany. He "made his picture, smoked his pipe, and waited." Decades later, he commanded the Allied forces to victory in WWI.
**Pattern**: *Vision precedes victory*. When a mental prototype is held without doubt, the creative law works through time and circumstance to fulfill it—often in ways the visualizer couldn't plan.

### Sample 4: The $500 Legacy
**Scenario**: An elderly woman, destitute and weary, had mentally pictured $500 for months. In court, a judge—unaware of her desire—paid her fine, then revealed he held $500 from a relative's estate belonging to her. Her picture had attracted the exact sum.
**Pattern**: *Faith bridges the gap between desire and delivery*. The law responds to the quality of your mental state, not your understanding of "how." Trust the process; details are handled by the Creative Power.

## Conclusion
To apply Invisible Power-thinking: choose one desire, form a clear mental picture of it *already fulfilled*, and hold it daily with cheerful confidence—without obsessing over "how." Then act on inspired opportunities as they arise. This isn't passive hoping; it's active co-creation with Universal Mind. In an age of distraction and doubt, Behrend's 1921 wisdom remains radical: you are not a victim of circumstance, but a center of creative operation. Remember her core promise: "The things I do, you can do." Start small. Trust the law. Watch your picture solidify.
