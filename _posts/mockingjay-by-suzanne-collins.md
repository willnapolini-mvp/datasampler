# MOCKINGJAY — Data Sampler Summary

## Core Insight
> True rebellion demands more than overthrowing tyrants—it requires rejecting the cycle of vengeance itself, or the revolution simply becomes the next oppression.

---

## Representative Samples: 4

### Sample 1: The Mockingjay Bargain
**Scenario**: President Coin asks Katniss to be the rebels' propaganda symbol. Katniss agrees—but only after negotiating terms: Peeta's immunity, her right to execute Snow, and her sister keeping their cat. She uses her symbolic value as leverage, not obedience.
**Pattern**: *Symbolic power is negotiable*. When you become a movement's face, your authenticity is the currency. Katniss teaches that influence isn't given—it's traded with clear boundaries.

### Sample 2: The Double Bomb at the Capitol Gates
**Scenario**: As rebels storm the Capitol, a first bomb hits child hostages. Medics rush to help—including Prim. A second bomb detonates, killing them all. Snow later reveals: Coin authorized the tactic to eliminate rivals and frame Snow.
**Pattern**: *Revolution mirrors the oppression it fights*. The method reveals the end. When rebels adopt the Capitol's cruelty, victory becomes indistinguishable from defeat.

### Sample 3: Peeta's Hijacking and Recovery
**Scenario**: The Capitol brainwashes Peeta to see Katniss as a threat. He attacks her, then slowly rebuilds his memory through "real or not real?" exercises with her patient presence.
**Pattern**: *Trauma distorts truth; trust rebuilds it*. Healing isn't about erasing pain but creating safety where reality can be tested and relearned—together.

### Sample 4: The Arrow That Changed Everything
**Scenario**: At Snow's execution, Katniss is handed the arrow to kill him. Instead, she shoots Coin—recognizing a new tyrant in revolutionary clothing. She accepts imprisonment to break the cycle.
**Pattern**: *Moral courage targets the system, not just the symbol*. The hardest rebellion is against the revolution you helped create.

---

## Key Generalizations

| Concept | Core Truth |
|---------|-----------|
| The Revolution Paradox | Overthrowing a tyrant with tyrannical methods installs a new tyrant. |
| Symbolic Leverage | Your value to a movement is your power—use it to protect what matters, not just to comply. |
| Trauma-Informed Action | Healing isn't passive recovery; it's the active choice to rebuild trust amid broken memories. |

## Formula
> **The Mockingjay Test**:  
> **Legitimacy = (Means × Intent) ÷ Cycle of Vengeance**  
> *When vengeance dominates, even righteous intent corrupts the outcome.*

---

## Conclusion
Apply Mockingjay-thinking: when joining any cause, audit the methods, not just the mission. Ask: "If we win using their tactics, what have we actually changed?" Protect your boundaries like Katniss negotiated hers. And remember—the most powerful act of rebellion isn't destroying the enemy, but refusing to become them. In an era of polarized movements, the person who breaks the cycle, not just the opponent, creates lasting change.
